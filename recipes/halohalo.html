Halo-Halo

Serving Size: 4-6 | Prep Time: 20-30 mins | Cooking Time: 0 mins | Total Time: 20-30 mins

Description

Halo-halo, which means "mix-mix" in Filipino, is a popular and colorful dessert enjoyed throughout the Philippines, especially during the hot summer months. It's a delightful concoction of various ingredients such as sweetened beans, fruits, jellies, shaved ice, and evaporated milk, topped with a scoop of ube (purple yam) ice cream or leche flan (caramel custard). This refreshing and indulgent dessert offers a symphony of flavors and textures, making each spoonful a delightful surprise. Halo-halo is not only a delicious treat but also a cultural icon that reflects the vibrant and diverse culinary heritage of the Philippines.

Ingredients

  • Shaved ice
  • Sweetened beans (red beans, chickpeas, etc.)
  • Sweetened fruits (jackfruit, banana, mango, etc.)
  • Nata de coco (coconut jelly)
  • Kaong (sugar palm fruit)
  • Macapuno (coconut sport)
  • Leche flan (caramel custard), sliced into cubes or scooped
  • Ube halaya (purple yam jam)
  • Pinipig (toasted young rice)
  • Evaporated milk
  • Ube ice cream (optional)
  • Additional toppings (optional): tapioca pearls, gulaman (gelatin cubes), shredded coconut

Cooking Directions

  1. Prepare Ingredients: Prepare the sweetened beans, fruits, nata de coco, kaong, and macapuno by draining any excess syrup or liquid. Slice the fruits into bite-sized pieces if necessary.
  2. Halo-halo: In a tall glass or bowl, start layering the ingredients. Begin with a layer of shaved ice at the bottom.
  3. Add Sweetened Beans and Fruits: Add a variety of sweetened beans and fruits on top of the shaved ice. Distribute them evenly to create a colorful and flavorful base.
  4. Layer Nata de Coco, Kaong, and Macapuno: Add a layer of nata de coco, kaong, and macapuno to the glass. These coconut-based ingredients add chewiness and texture to the halo-halo.
  5. Add Leche Flan and Ube Halaya: Place slices of leche flan on top of the coconut ingredients. Spoon some ube halaya over the leche flan layer. These rich and creamy components add decadence to the dessert.
  6. Sprinkle with Pinipig: Sprinkle toasted pinipig (young rice) over the top of the halo-halo for a crunchy texture.
  7. Pour Evaporated Milk: Generously pour evaporated milk over the assembled halo-halo to moisten the ingredients and add creaminess.
  8. Top with Ube Ice Cream (Optional): For an extra indulgent touch, add a scoop of ube ice cream on top of the halo-halo.
  9. Garnish (Optional): Optionally, you can garnish the halo-halo with additional toppings such as tapioca pearls, gulaman, or shredded coconut for added texture and flavor.
  10. Serve and Enjoy: Serve the halo-halo immediately with a long spoon or a straw for mixing and enjoying all the layers of flavors and textures.
